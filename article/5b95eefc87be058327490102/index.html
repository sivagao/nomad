Best Hotels, Hostels, & Resorts

iStock.com/anyaberkut

So we may be a bit biased, but we think everyone should visit Chiang Mai for a few days if they’ve come all the way to Thailand. Since we’ve lived in this tiny town in Northern Thailand for the past five years, we’ve had our fair share of friends, family members, and readers who want to know what to see, what to eat, and where to stay in Chiang Mai. Well, we’ve finally gotten around to answer the last question!

Full disclosure: we have a house and don’t bounce from hotel to hotel like many other travel bloggers do. However, we still keep a running list of the accommodations we’ve stayed at, friends have told us about, or we’ve learned through the grapevine and were impressed by what we heard. We pay attention to details like location, atmosphere, the helpfulness of the staff, and the value for money before deciding where the best place to stay are and sharing our finds.

Below you’ll find a short but solid list of best resorts, hostels, guesthouses, and hotels in Chiang Mai of all sorts of styles and budgets. We’ve also included a map (at the bottom of the post) so you can easily see where they are in relation to other parts of the city. Enjoy!

Where to Stay in Chiang Mai on a Budget

Chiang Mai is extremely budget-friendly and has an incredible selection of hostels and guesthouses that range from as little as 5 USD per night upwards to around 30 USD per night. It’s a nice change of pace if you’re coming from the more expensive areas of the country like Bangkok or the islands. In this price range, you can expect to get a small but clean room, a shared lounging area, and usually some complimentary tea and snacks. Here are our top picks for where to stay in Chiang Mai on a budget:


Heuan Pak-Dee [email protected] is great value for money and its location near one of the most popular temples in the city can’t get much better. The hostel’s friendly staff is happy to make recommendations for things to do around the city and they’ll help to arrange for tours and transportation, too. Decorated with colorful Thai accents, we really like that it offers both private rooms and co-ed and female only dorms, which is a great option for solo female travelers.

Nearest major landmark: Phra Singh Temple
Room types: Twins, King, Dorms (co-ed and female only)
Amenities: Bicycles, breakfast, wifi, garden

Read reviews for Heuan Pak-Dee [email protected] on Agoda or Booking.com

The Peaberry


Located in the upper right-hand corner of the Old City, we love that it’s easy to reach both the main part of town and the modern Nimman area if you stay at The Peaberry. It has clean, bright rooms decorated with Northern Thai Lanna décor, free bicycles to tour the city with, and a nice little garden seating area outdoors. We also noticed it was a good choice for families traveling with a small child or two because they have family suites.

Nearest major landmark: Chiang Mai RAM Hospital
Room types: Double, Singles, Family Suite
Amenities: Free wifi, free breakfast, free bicycles

Read reviews for The Peaberry on Agoda or Booking.com

Chiang Maan Residence


Chiang Maan Residence is a Thai-styled serviced apartment that features cozy gardens and rustic Northern Thai decorations. It’s part of a larger house and the hospitality from the owners (who live on site) make it feel like you’re visiting longtime friends. It’s also right next to the city’s oldest temple, Wat Chiang Man. The area is quiet at night, is within walking distance of numerous cafés and shops, and is just steps away from public transportation.

Nearest major landmark: Chiang Man Temple
Room type: Double
Amenities: Wifi, bicycles

Read reviews for Chiang Maan Residence on Agoda or Booking.com

Mid-Range Places to Stay in Chiang Mai

For not much more than you’d spend on a nice dinner back home (between 50 to 100 USD), you can get a really comfortable but stylish hotel or guesthouse in Chiang Mai. You can usually get a complimentary breakfast and airport shuttle and other perks like laundry services or a pool to cool off in. There are several chain hotels and big brands in town, but (shhh!) but we think the best places are the charming boutique hotels that line the quiet streets in the Old City. These are our recommendations for where to stay in Chiang Mai if you want a balance between price and comfort:

Moon Dragon Hotel


Moon Dragon Hotel is situated right on the Sunday Night Walking Street in the center of the Old City, so it’s in the middle of the action and in close proximity to many shops and cafés. Its rooms have ample sunlight and are decorated in a sleek but serene Asian décor. We like that after enjoying a day out on town, we could come back and enjoy a cool dip in the room’s private pool and relax on our room’s balcony.

Nearest major landmark: Thapae Gate
Room types: Twins, King, Suite, Studio
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool

Read reviews for Moon Dragon Hotel on Agoda or Booking.com

Thapae Loft Hotel


Ever since it was built a couple of years ago, we’ve been interested in Thapae Loft every time we drive by. It has a sleek industrial look that’s tucked away in a quiet side street in city center. It’s not far from Thapae Gate (a major landmark in the city) and just a minute’s walk from the must-visit Sunday Night Walking Street. It also has several amenities we particularly enjoy in a hotel like on-site laundry, a pool, a restaurant, and a café.

Nearest major landmark: Thai Coin Museum
Room types: Twins, King, Triple
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool, airport shuttle, on-site laundry

Read reviews for Thapae Loft Hotel on Agoda or Booking.com

U Nimman Chiang Mai


Less than a 10-minute drive from the airport, U Nimman Chiang Mai is in the posh Nimmanhaemin area just west of Chiang Mai’s Old City. Restaurants, cafés, and boutique shops are just steps away from the hotel’s front doors as are easy-to-hail tuk-tuks that will allow you to venture elsewhere in the city. We particularly love the swim-up bar at their rooftop pool, which overlooks the city’s charming skyline.

Nearest major landmark: MAYA Lifestyle Mall
Room types: Twins, King, One-Bedroom Suite, Two-Bedroom Suite
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool, massage and spa, gym, free bicycles

Read reviews for U Nimman on Agoda or Booking.com

Luxurious Places to Stay in Chiang Mai

Can you take a guest at what a luxury resort or villa in Chiang Mai costs? Well, let’s just say you can get a fantastic room starting at 150+ USD night. Although the five-star standards aren’t quite high as you’d find in, say, Europe, it’s incredible what you can get on that type of money. You can look forward to taking a dip in a beautiful pool, enjoying an incredible breakfast spread, indulging in a spa, and more. Read on for where to stay in Chiang Mai if you want to treat yourself:

Anantara Chiang Mai Resort


This five-star Thai colonial resort is situated right on the Ping River near the heart of Chiang Mai Old City. It’s also walking distance of the Anusarn Night Market where there are Thai souvenirs, live music, and great places to eat galore. Anantara holds itself to incredibly high standards and offers guests exceptional service whether you’re a honeymooning couple or a family with young children. The outdoor pool is gorgeous, they have an award-winning spa and massage service, a swanky bar, and one of the best restaurants (The Service 1921) in town.

Nearest major landmark: Anusarn Market in the Night Bazaar
Room types: Twins, King, Suite
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool, massage and spa, garden, shuttle

Read reviews for Anantara Chiang Mai Resort on Agoda or Booking.com

Tamarind Village


Surrounded by a series of manicured courtyards, Tamarind Village is a quiet, secluded escape from the city, even though it’s in the city. This boutique hotel’s rooms are absolutely drool-worthy with their warm, modern-meets-rustic décor that reflect the colors and patterns of the local hill tribe communities of Northern Thailand. We just love love love TAmarind Village and reserve it for special occasions. Plus, who could turn down a cool dip in their pool near a stunning garden wall or their exceptional service at the onsite bar and restaurant.

Nearest major landmark: Anusarn Market in the Night Bazaar
Room types: Twins, King, Suite
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool, gym

Read reviews for Tamarind Village on Agoda or Booking.com

Amata Lanna Chiang Mai Hotel


Classic Northern Thai décor, rich teak wood accents, and Buddha statues complete the feel of being in a Thai tropical oasis at the Amata Lanna Chiang Mai Hotel. It’s hard to choose a favorite place to relax – the room’s scenic balcony, at the hotel’s bar enjoying a fancy cocktail, or lounging outside at the pool. Situated between the Night Bazaar and the Ping River, it’s within walking distance of Chinatown, Warorot Market, and the Ping River, which are all places worth visiting.

Nearest major landmark: Wat Chai Mongkhon near Ping River
Room types: Twins, King, Suite
Amenities: Breakfast, wifi, pool, garden

Read reviews for Amata Lanna Chiang Mai Hotel on Agoda or Booking.com

BONUS

Baan Boo Loo


If you’re looking for something truly unique, Baan Boo Loo is the place to stay in Chiang Mai. It’s a far cry from chain hotels because it’s several traditional Thai-style stilted bungalows nestled in a jungle garden. The rooms are incredible too, with their rustic-meets-elegance vibe opening up to look at the gardens below. We also love that the owners of Baan Boo Loo take extra care to make sure your stay is outstanding, from cooking special breakfasts to helping you get a tuk-tuk ride around the Old City.

Nearest major landmark: Wat Phra Singh
Room types: Rooms, suites, and villas
Amenities: Breakfast, tuk-tuk driver, airport pick up

Read reviews for Baan Boo Loo on Agoda or Booking.com
