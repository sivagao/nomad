From Desk Jockey to Digital Nomad in One Year – Jessica Guzik – Medium

From Desk Jockey to Digital Nomad in One Year

I am what a lot of the Internet would call a digital nomad. In my case, I live abroad and make money by working remotely for several clients.

I first read about digital nomads in 2014, while I was still working a desk job. I fantasized about being able to leave the fluorescent fishbowl of my office, go forth into the world, pack minimalistically, and work under palm fronds. Indeed, if you look up anything about digital nomads, you’ll find blogs with gorgeous pictures of Indonesia and Thailand and young people who somehow are able to travel the world without getting UTIs.

Meanwhile, like @cuisine_etcetera, we’re stuck in front of a computer in someone else’s office, Googling how late we can wait before we have to freeze our eggs. (PS: Not very long, I have to make that decision soon).

Nowadays when I read about how people became digital nomads, I feel like the most popular stories aren’t the most realistic. I want to give you a month-by-month breakdown of how I went from someone with a full-time job and an employer-sponsored health plan to someone who is:

  • Living in Berlin, Germany;
  • Definitely not making enough money to live full-time yet;
  • Not sure what I’m going do with my law school loans;
  • Single;
  • And still Googling how to freeze my eggs.

That is the dream, no?

Because I moved to Germany in March 2017, I’ll start with March 2016. And here’s a video version of this story (it’s a little different):

March 2016

The job that I had at the time was a good job but it didn’t involve any writing. I missed that creative outlet. So in March 2016, I started writing on Medium. I remember my first story here had five likes — and I was really excited by those five likes (one of them was definitely my mom).

Lesson: Write on Medium, duh.

April 2016

As I continued to write on Medium, you could see my interest in digital nomadism creeping into my work. For example I wrote a piece about whether you should become a digital nomad or clean your room. I was trying to convince myself that I could scratch the itch by cleaning my room. It turns out I was wrong.

Lesson: Suppressed desires can be bellwethers of the future

May 2016

In May, I’ll admit that I coasted. Honestly, I met a guy in a coffee shop whom I was all aflutter about. For a while, I wasn’t focused on building a writing portfolio or a career that freed me to travel the world. I was back to thinking that maybe living in my hometown of Washington, DC wasn’t so bad.

Lesson: It’s OK to give yourself a break, even those breaks come unexpectedly and/or in the form of men who surprise you with hyacinths.

June 2016

Guess what happened with Mr. Hyacinth? The flowers outlasted the budding relationship!

When things ended, I rented a Car2Go, locked myself inside, rolled up the windows, and I screamed as loud as I could. And it felt great! So I screamed as loud as I could three more times.

I was so frustrated: I couldn’t make relationships work and I wasn’t sure what I wanted to do professionally in the long run. I felt like I was at a standstill. After I finished screaming, I started fantasizing.

I asked myself: What am I going after here? What’s the life that I really want?

I kept returning to this fantasy of being a digital nomad and traveling more. As part of that, I became interested in co-living. I took a shining to the idea of living in a house in Spain with other entrepreneurs, or small palace a place in Bangkok with a few fellow creatives.

As I peruse co-living spaces, I discovered WeLive. They had just opened a co-living space in Crystal City, Virginia, which is a suburb of Washington DC. But why would I live in a co-living space in Washington DC? It’s my hometown and I already had a nice one-bedroom apartment. But then I thought, if I’m all about exploring this lifestyle, then moving into a local co-living community might be a good way to see if I really like living with less stuff and living around lots of people while not giving up my full-time job.

Lesson: Cars really are soundproof!

July 2016

In July I moved into WeLive, and it was fucking awesome!

Before I moved, I organized a “Pillage Party” to sell all my stuff to friends. One of the coolest things is that now, whenever I visit friends, I get to see parts of my former life in their apartments. It’s like I’m still connected to them through the stuff that I got rid of.

When I started writing about WeLive, a particular contact reached out on Twitter and suggested that she and I get drinks. She worked in Crystal City, where WeLive was located, and her job was to promote Crystal City community.

When we got together for drinks, this contact started talking about how she wanted to build Crystal City’s sense of community through dinner clubs. Well, it turns out that in 2012, I had started a dinner club series designed to foster connections among strangers.

So I said, “Well hey, I’ve done that before.”

And she said “Oh, would you do it in Crystal City?”

And I said, “Uh, would you pay me?”

And she said, “Yeah.”

And I thought: Well, goddamn. I’m an entrepreneur now!

Lesson: Always ask for money!!!!!

August 2016

Guess what: I met another boy!

Once again, I shoved all my interests aside and thought, “This guy’s great! Screw traveling, I’ll just stay here. I can build a nice life — I like DC!” I was still doing Curated Table. I was still writing. But I definitely wasn’t chomping at the bit to become a digital nomad.

Lesson: It’s OK to give yourself a break, even those breaks come unexpectedly and/or in the form of men who surprise you with beach vacations.

September 2016

The guy and I split!

This time I didn’t scream into a rental car, so that was a step in the right direction. But I did have another soul-searching moment. I had gotten rid of my stuff, I was growing a profitable business, and I was really loving writing. What now?

At this point I got a bonus from my full-time job. It wasn’t a huge payload, but it was enough to give me some financial padding to play with.

I took a look at my professional, personal, and financial situation and had one thought: I gotta go.

Lesson: Finances matter. So does luck.

October 2016

Even though I had made the decision to go, I tortured myself for a month fretting over which direction to take. I was torn because, while my side hustle was going well, I had also always wanted to travel long-term (which by American standards means over one month).

While I was weighing my options, I was not bringing my best self to work. That’s how I accidentally quit my job. I was having a bad day and my manager asked me about it:

I just couldn’t keep it inside anymore. On October 24th, 2016 I quit my full-time job. I have not been full-time employed ever since.

Lesson: At a certain point, planning is futile. Something in our subconscious will cause us to act (out) when the time is right.

November 2016

I finally decided to go full-throttle with my side hustle AND to travel.

I booked a flight to Paris and back from New Year’s Eve through Valentine’s Day. My plan was to go to Paris for a couple of days, and then to au pair in Rome for a month. I bragged about my plan to everyone who would listen.

Lesson: You can have your cake and eat it too.

December 2016

The bragging continued into December 2016. I was sitting pretty: I had a growing business, an awesome travel plan, and affordable health insurance (thanks, The Obama White House!). And then I left.

Lesson: Enjoy the good times!

January 2017

I left for Europe and celebrated an epic New Year’s Eve in Paris. After a few days in Paris, I headed to Rome to move in with my au pair family.

It turns out I’m not cut out for au pairing. I wrestled with the decision, and I eventually decided to leave. I only had seven weeks in Europe (I thought at the time), and I didn’t want to waste them being miserable. I made arrangements to leave the family, and then to leave Rome. And then my trip took a turn for the amazing.
